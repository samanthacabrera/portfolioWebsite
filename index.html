minimal style
I believe that less is more when it comes to website design. Keeping the website uncluttered and simple allows the user to easily interact with the content -- ensuring a pleasant experience.

purposeful design
Each element of a webpage should be delibrate and serve a purpose. This helps to avoid clutter, and allows the developer to focus solely on beneficial features.

artistic compostion
An outdated or unpleasing webpage reflects poorly on the webpage owner or company. By keeping a website modern, you show the user that you value their time and dedicated to remaining at the top.

My Process

| inspiration |

To stay relevant in an ever-evolving industry, I keep up to date on modern website design. I do this by following pages that highlight web design techniques and ideas on pinterest and instagram. This highlights aestetics, technologies, and methodologies that I can learn and practice.

| research |

When I begin a new project, I first gather all available information regarding the project. For example, I get an idea of what the clients wants, gather any pictures or data to be display, and then I start making design decisions.

| execution |

When working on a project, I have learned to apply proper design principles throughout the project, and not just at the end. I complete the functionality of each component before moving on. I have learned that this saves time as it avoids bugs later on.
